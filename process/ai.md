# Guidance on AI tools

## Key princples 

- AI is a _magnifier_. It takes what the organisation does well and magnifies it. It takes what the organisation does poorly and magnifies it.
- AI pointed at the wrong problem is not neutral but negative. We get faster at the wrong thing.
- AI is _transformational_. Ask what's now possible to do that we could not earlier? This leads to transformational outcomes. Using AI to do what we always did, but faster, only leads to incremental outcomes. (As an aside, this was one of the lessons from the book, [The art of doing science and engineering](https://www.amazon.co.uk/Art-Doing-Science-Engineering-Learning/dp/1732265178)).
- AI excels at _pattern matching across domains_. Innovation often emerges from such cross-pollination. Humans tend to look for answers from the same domain as the questions. AI has no such bias.

## Use AI

- To solve a problem we could not solve before, either because we did not have the expertise, or the _courage_ to undertake it due to the effort required.
- To deliver work at a better _baseline_ quality, not merely faster.  
- To nurture child-like curiosity by asking questions we did not know whom to ask before
- To increase our own talent density with more intentional practise of our craft

## Software development with AI coding agents

Software development practises fit for humans also happen to be fit for LLMs. Good abstractions, low coupling/high cohesion, DRY, SOLID, comments and docs are all quite important still, as they also provide high quality _context_ that AI agents need to generate good quality code. Continue to document the _what_, the _why_ and initial design of interface descriptions and behaviours. No AI can guess out-of-context assumptions and constraints simply by looking at the code.
